\section{Mathematical Framework}
A single resonance is modeled as $R(t) = A e^{\gamma t}$, $\gamma > 0$. 
The alternating series of such resonances is
\begin{equation}
\Xi(t) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n A_n e^{\gamma_n t}.
\end{equation}
For identical $A$ and $\gamma$, with periodic sign switching of period $T$,
\begin{equation}
\Xi(t) = A e^{\gamma t} \cdot \operatorname{sgn}(\sin(2\pi t/T)).
\end{equation}
The moving average over window $T$ satisfies
\begin{equation}
\frac{1}{T} \int_{t-T/2}^{t+T/2} \Xi(\tau)\,d\tau \approx 0,
\end{equation}
with an exponentially small residual $O(e^{\gamma t}/ (\gamma T)^2)$ for large $T$.
